The New York University Center for Data Science is collecting your answers for use in research on computer understanding of English. Thank you for your help!

For this task, you will be shown a pair of prompts, where one is written by a human and another one is generated by a machine. Your job is to figure out, based on a correct prompt (S1), if another prompt (S2) is also correct:

  1. The order of the prompts is not fixed; the human-written sentence can appear either as S1 or S2.
  2. In some cases, S1 and S2 can be very similar. Please pay attention to this kind of pair since the correct label will depend on the minimal differences between S1 and S2.

You need to choose one out of the four labels:

  • Choose definitely correct  if any event or situation that can be described by S1 would also fit S2.
    Example:
    S1: "Venus has certain similarities to Earth which, if not for the hostile conditions, might make colonization easier in many respects in comparison with other possible destinations."
    S2: "Venus has some similarities with the Earth."
    Our explanation: S2 contains information that is stated in S1.

  • Choose maybe correct if S2 could describe an event or situation that fit S1, but could also describe sentences that don't fit S1.
    Example:
    S1: "This testimony brought him to the attention of other Americans in London."
    S2: "This testimony inspired other Americans in London."
    Our explanation: To bring attention does not necessarily mean to inspire.

  • Choose definitely incorrect if any event or situation that could possibly be described with S1 would not fit S2. 
    Example:
    S1: "She acted quickly and invited him to visit Mary in Buda Castle."
    S2: "He acted quickly and invited her to visit Mary in Buda Castle.”
    Our explanation: We focus on the correct event in S1, therefore there should only be one person (she) who invited the other person (he).

  • Choose I don't understand if there is something wrong with the prompts that make them hard to understand (more than just a typo), maybe because of some machine-generated errors.
    Example:
    S1: “External link in |publisher= (help) Balanis, Constantine A. (2011).”
    S2: “Accessed on April 29, 2013.”
    Our explanation: S1 is not a sentence.
